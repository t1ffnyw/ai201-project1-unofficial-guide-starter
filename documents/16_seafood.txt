[player_9, 47 pts] Thames St is a good all-rounder; great food, but not fancy, cool ambiance, but not over-the-top, good service, seems like they hire good industry people. Hip and authentic, not pretentious. Just a solid Baltimore seafood joint. 

http://www.thamesstreetoysterhouse.com

IMO the negatives are - small space gets crowded quickly, tough to get a table at peak times, can get expensive (although I think the quality generally reflects the price here).

Also a good spot to bring visitors because of its location. When I have friends/family come into town, Fells is my favorite neighborhood to take them first.

For a more “intimate” place, Dillon’s Oyster in Hampden is good, and this is a good time of year to explore the rest of 36th St Ave, and the holiday lights nearby on 34th St, all in Hampden. Also close by , Druid Hill Park is doing a Christmas event that you can take your car through after dinner at Dillon’s. 

https://dylansoyster.com

https://bcrp.baltimorecity.gov/charmcitylights

Whatever you do, have fun and happy holidays!
